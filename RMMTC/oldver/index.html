Conference Overview

The Biomedical Engineering Society at the University of Colorado at Boulder is pleased to invite you to the Rocky Mountain Medical Technologies Conference. Held on Monday, February 25, 2013 at the University Memorial Center on CU's campus, the conference objectives are to:

Provide a forum where professionals, companies, physicians, and students may network and promote the field of biomedical engineering, both in industry and academia.

Establish the Rocky Mountain region as a leader in medical technologies.

Provide participants the opportunity to listen to speeches and participate in workshops from some of the nation's top leaders in the field.

Provide the opportunity for both industry and academia to showcase the latest advances in medical technologies.

Educate younger participants about the field of biomedical engineering and promote involvement through a variety of activities.

Who Should Attend?

The conference is open to the general public, but is specifically geared toward:

College students and faculty from universities in the Rocky Mountain region

Companies in biomedical engineering, biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and medical technologies

Hospital Administrators and Physicians - surgeons, specialists, general practitioners, pharmacists, physical therapists

Industry professionals

High school students from the front range of Colorado
